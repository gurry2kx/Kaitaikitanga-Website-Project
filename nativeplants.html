Kōwhai

Known for their brilliant yellow flowers that burst into bloom during spring, Kōwhai trees are an iconic symbol of New Zealand. They are a crucial food source for native birds like the Tūī and Kererū.

Ponga (Silver Fern)

The Silver Fern is famous worldwide as a symbol of New Zealand identity. Its unique fronds feature a distinctive silvery-white underside that reflects light beautifully in the dark forest understory.

Harakeke (Flax)

Harakeke is one of New Zealand's most versatile native plants. Traditionally used by Māori for weaving clothing, mats, and ropes, it also plays an essential role in stabilizing soil and waterways.

Pōhutukawa

Often referred to as the New Zealand Christmas tree, the Pōhutukawa puts on a spectacular display of crimson red flowers along coastal cliffs every December, holding deep cultural significance.

Tī Kōuka (Cabbage Tree)

Tī Kōuka is a resilient native tree found across many landscapes. With its long, tough leaves and tall trunks, it flourishes in wetlands and open fields, providing valuable nesting habitats for native wildlife.
